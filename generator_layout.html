Formal complaints

In some situations you may want to or may be required to provide information about a formal complaints procedures. For example, a quality management process within your organization may require you to establish a formal complaints procedure with clear escalation paths. The EU Web Accessibility Directive {% include_cached external.html %} foresees EU countries to have legal “enforcement procedures” when users are not satisfied with the responses from public bodies. Inform users about any such formal complaints procedures. This may also motivate them to provide you with valuable feedback about the accessibility of your content.

Example: “We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 2 business days, and to propose a solution within 10 business days. You are entitled to escalate a complaint to [organizational or legal entity], should you be dissatisfied with our response to you.”
