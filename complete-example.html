Accessibility Statement for Example Store

Example Inc. is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone, and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

Measures to support accessibility

Example Inc. takes the following measures to ensure accessibility of Example Store:

  • Integrate accessibility into our procurement practices.
  • Provide continual accessibility training for our staff.
  • Include people with disabilities in our design personas
  • Include people with disabilities in our usability testing
  • Meet third-party certification requirements

Conformance status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) have three conformance levels: A, AA, and AAA. These guidelines provide criteria to make the content more accessible for people with disabilities and improve the user experience for everyone. Example Store is Partially conformant with the WCAG 2.1 Level AA standard. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.

Additions to the requirements

Although our goal is WCAG 2.1 Level AA conformance, we have also applied some Level AAA Success Criteria: Images of text are only used for decorative purposes. Re-authentication after a session expires does not cause loss of data. Some videos have sign language interpretation.

Compatibility with browsers and assistive technology

Example Store is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • browser X with assistive technology Y on operating system Z
  • browser X with assistive technology Y on operating system Z
  • browser X with assistive technology Y on operating system Z

Example Store is not compatible with:

  • browsers older than 3 major versions" or "Mobile operating systems older than 5 years
  • browsers older than 3 major versions" or "Mobile operating systems older than 5 years

Technical specifications

The technologies Example Store uses may affect the usability. This also depends on the combination of assistive technologies, browsers and plugins that are used. We used the following technologies to design accessibility features of Example Store:

  • HTML
  • WAI-ARIA
  • CSS
  • Javascript

These technologies are relied upon for conformance.

Limitations and alternatives

Despite our best efforts to ensure accessibility of Example Store, there may be some limitations. Below is a description of known limitations, and potential solutions and alternatives to employ. Please contact us if an issue you experience is not listed below.

Known limitations for Example Store:

  • Comments from users: Uploaded images may not have text alternatives because We cannot ensure the quality of contributions. We monitor user comments and typically repair issues within 2 work days. Please use the 'report issue' button if you encounter an issue.
  • Archived documents: Might not work with current assistive technologies because Use outdated technologies that do not support accessibility. We convert documents to new formats upon request within 5 working days. Please contact documents@example.org for support.
